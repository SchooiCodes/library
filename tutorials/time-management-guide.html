Eisenhower Matrix

The Eisenhower Matrix (also called the Urgent-Important Matrix) helps prioritize tasks:

  • Quadrant 1 (Do First): Urgent + Important — Crises, deadlines
  • Quadrant 2 (Schedule): Not Urgent + Important — Planning, learning, exercise
  • Quadrant 3 (Delegate): Urgent + Not Important — Interruptions, meetings
  • Quadrant 4 (Eliminate): Not Urgent + Not Important — Time wasters

Spend most of your time in Quadrant 2 for long-term success.

Time Blocking

Time Blocking means scheduling every hour of your day in advance. This prevents context switching and ensures deep work time.

Example day:

  • 7-9 AM: Deep work (most important task)
  • 9-10 AM: Email and messages
  • 10-12 PM: Meetings
  • 12-1 PM: Lunch break
  • 1-3 PM: Creative work
  • 3-4 PM: Admin tasks
  • 4-5 PM: Planning tomorrow

GTD Method

Getting Things Done (GTD) by David Allen has 5 steps:

  1. Capture — Collect everything in an inbox
  2. Clarify — Process each item: what is it? is it actionable?
  3. Organize — Put into lists: Next Actions, Waiting, Someday/Maybe, Calendar
  4. Reflect — Weekly review to update lists
  5. Engage — Work through Next Actions list by context

Weekly Reviews

A weekly review is the cornerstone of GTD and time management. Set aside 30-60 minutes each week:

  1. Process all inboxes to zero
  2. Review next actions lists
  3. Check calendar for upcoming events
  4. Review Someday/Maybe list
  5. Plan the upcoming week
  6. Clear your desk and digital space

Digital Calendars

Recommended calendar tools:

  • Google Calendar — Free, integrates everywhere
  • Fantastical — Mac/iOS premium calendar
  • Notion Calendar — Integrated with Notion workspaces
  • Outlook Calendar — Best for corporate environments

Pro tip: Color-code calendars by category (work, personal, health, learning).
