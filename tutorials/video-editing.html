Video Editing

Video editing — DaVinci Resolve, Premiere Pro, color grading, and motion graphics.

Table of Contents (2 sections)

  • Editing Workflow
  • Color Grading & Export

Editing Workflow

Non-linear editing (NLE): timeline-based. DaVinci Resolve: professional-grade, free version — cut page, edit page, fusion (motion graphics/effects), color page, fairlight (audio), deliver (export). Premiere Pro: industry standard, subscription — dynamic link with After Effects, Audition. Timeline: tracks (video V1-Vn, audio A1-An), razor (cut), ripple edit (shift clips), rolling edit, slip, slide. Editing: import > organize bins > rough cut > fine cut > add transitions > color grade > audio mix > export. Key shortcuts: C (razor), V (select), B (trim), Ctrl+K (cut clip). Transitions: cut (hard), dissolve, dip to black, L-cut (audio leads video), J-cut (video leads audio). Speed: slow motion (optical flow interpolation), time remapping, speed ramps. Multicam: sync multiple angles, switch in real-time.

Color Grading & Export

Color correction: exposure, contrast, white balance, saturation — primary corrections. Color wheels: lift (shadows), gamma (midtones), gain (highlights). Curves: precise tonal control. Log footage: flat color profile (S-Log, V-Log, LOG C) — more dynamic range. LUTs (Look-Up Tables): creative color transforms. DaVinci Resolve: nodes (serial, parallel, layer), Power Windows (masks — circular, linear, gradient), tracker, color match, HDR grading. Scopes: waveform, vectorscope, histogram, parade. Export: codec (H.264/H.265 for web, ProRes/DNxHD for mastering), bitrate (VBR/CBR), resolution (1080p, 4K, 8K), frame rate (24fps film, 30fps TV, 60fps sports). Keyframes: effects animation, position, scale, rotation, opacity. Motion graphics: After Effects or Fusion — text animation, lower thirds, particle systems. Proxy workflow: edit with low-res proxies, switch to full-res for final render.
