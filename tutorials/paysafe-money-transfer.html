Paysafe & Online Money Transfer Guide

A practical guide to using Paysafe (paysafecard) for online payments, along with a comparison of alternatives like PayPal, Wise, Skrill, and Revolut for different use cases.

What is Paysafe?

Paysafe (formerly known as paysafecard) is a prepaid online payment method that lets you pay on the internet without needing a bank account, credit card, or sharing any personal financial information. It works using a 16-digit PIN code printed on a voucher that you buy with cash at physical retail locations.

Paysafe is particularly popular for:

  • Online gaming and gambling deposits
  • Purchasing from sites that do not accept credit cards
  • Privacy-conscious shopping (no financial data shared)
  • Teens and young adults without bank accounts
  • People who prefer not to use credit cards online

Paysafe operates in over 50 countries with 650,000+ point-of-sale locations worldwide. You can buy vouchers in denominations ranging from €10 to €100 (or local equivalent). The maximum you can store in a single Paysafe account is €5,000.

One key limitation: Paysafe is primarily a one-way payment method. You cannot receive refunds to it easily, and you generally cannot withdraw funds from a Paysafe account back to your bank. For this reason, it is best used for spending, not receiving or storing money long-term.

Setting Up an Account

While you can use paysafecard vouchers without an account (just enter the 16-digit PIN at checkout), creating a "myPaysafe" account unlocks additional features like combining multiple vouchers and splitting payments.

How to create a myPaysafe account:

  1. Go to paysafe.com and click "Register".
  2. Enter your email address and create a password.
  3. Select your country of residence.
  4. Verify your email via the confirmation link sent to your inbox.
  5. Complete your profile (name, date of birth, address).
  6. Optionally, verify your identity for higher limits (requires ID upload).

Benefits of an account:

  • Combine multiple voucher balances into one account
  • Split payments across multiple vouchers for large purchases
  • View transaction history
  • Lock your account if your voucher PIN is compromised
  • Link to Skrill/NETELLER for withdrawal options

Limits without verification: Maximum account balance of €250 / £200 / $300. Annual spending limit of €2,500 / £2,000. Verification raises these limits significantly.

Depositing Funds

There are several ways to add funds to your Paysafe account:

Buying vouchers at retail locations

This is the original method. Visit a participating store (gas stations, convenience stores, supermarkets) and ask for a paysafecard. You pay cash for a voucher with a printed 16-digit PIN. Denominations vary by country but typically range from €10 to €100.

Finding a vendor: Use the store finder on the Paysafe website or app. Enter your location to see nearby sellers.

Buying vouchers online

In some countries, you can purchase digital paysafecards online via bank transfer, credit card, or other payment methods. The PIN is delivered digitally by email or in your account dashboard.

Top-up via bank transfer (selected countries)

Some European countries support direct bank transfer to your myPaysafe account. This is convenient but defeats the anonymity purpose of using Paysafe.

Entering the PIN

  1. Log into your myPaysafe account.
  2. Click "Add PIN" or "Top up".
  3. Enter the 16-digit PIN from your voucher.
  4. The amount is immediately added to your account balance.

You can also use the voucher directly at checkout without an account — simply select paysafecard as payment method and enter the PIN when prompted.

Using Paysafe Online

Using Paysafe for online purchases is straightforward. Here is how it works at different types of merchants:

At supported merchants

  1. Go to checkout on a supported website.
  2. Select "paysafecard" as your payment method.
  3. Enter the amount to pay.
  4. You will be redirected to Paysafe's secure payment page.
  5. Enter your 16-digit PIN (or log into myPaysafe to use your balance).
  6. Payment is deducted instantly.

Paysafe as intermediary

Some services like Skrill and NETELLER allow you to deposit using paysafecard. This means you can:

  • Buy a paysafecard voucher with cash
  • Deposit into Skrill/NETELLER using the voucher
  • Use Skrill/NETELLER to send money to bank accounts, withdraw to card, or transfer to other users
  • This effectively turns cash into digital money that can be moved online

This is a popular method for online gamblers, freelancers who get paid in vouchers, and people who want to convert cash to digital currency without a bank account.

Paysafe at gaming/gambling sites

Paysafe is the most popular deposit method at online casinos, sportsbooks, and poker sites. Deposits are instant, and many sites offer deposit bonuses specifically for paysafecard users. Withdrawals typically go to Skrill or bank transfer instead.

Important: Some gambling sites restrict withdrawals to the same method as the deposit. Since paysafecard does not support withdrawals, you may need an alternative e-wallet. Always check the site's withdrawal policy before depositing.

Alternatives

Depending on your needs, one of these alternatives may be more suitable than Paysafe. Each excels in different scenarios.

PayPal

The most widely accepted online payment system. Buyer protection for purchases, instant transfers between users, linked to credit/debit cards. High fees for international transfers and currency conversion.

Best for: General online shopping, eBay, sending money to friends (within same country)

Fees: 2.99% for goods/services, 5.99% for international, free for friends/family (bank funded)

Wise (formerly TransferWise)

Best for international money transfers. Uses real mid-market exchange rates with transparent fees. Supports 50+ currencies. Multi-currency account with debit card. Lower fees than banks and PayPal.

Best for: International transfers, freelancers receiving foreign payments, travel

Fees: 0.41-1.5% depending on route, always transparent

Skrill / NETELLER

E-wallets popular in the gambling and gaming industry. Instant deposits to casinos. Skrill supports paysafecard deposits. Both offer prepaid Mastercards. Higher fees than alternatives for inactive accounts.

Best for: Online gambling, gaming, international transfers to other users

Fees: 1.45% deposit (card), 5.50% withdrawal, €4.50/mo inactivity fee after 12 months

Revolut

Multi-currency digital banking app. Exchange between 30+ currencies at interbank rates, crypto trading, stock trading, budgeting tools. Free tier includes local account details in multiple currencies.

Best for: Multi-currency spending, travel, daily banking with mobile-first experience

Fees: Free basic tier, Premium $9.99/mo, Metal $16.99/mo. Most currency exchange fee-free up to $1,000/mo

Security Tips

While Paysafe is generally secure because it does not expose your financial details, there are important precautions to take.

Best Practices

  • Only buy paysafecard vouchers from official retail partners listed on the Paysafe website
  • Scratch off the PIN coating only when you are ready to use it
  • Never share your 16-digit PIN with anyone
  • Use a myPaysafe account to combine small vouchers into a protected balance
  • Check your Paysafe balance regularly at paysafe.com
  • Enable two-factor authentication on your myPaysafe account
  • Keep the receipt from your voucher purchase in case of issues

Common Scams & Pitfalls

  • Fake voucher sellers: Only buy from authorized retailers. Online sellers offering discounts are likely scams.
  • Phishing emails: Emails claiming to be from Paysafe asking for your PIN are always scams. Paysafe never asks for your PIN.
  • Social engineering: People asking you to read them your PIN for "verification" — never comply.
  • Non-delivery scams: On marketplaces, buyers paying with a used/vacant paysafecard PIN. Always verify payment clears before delivering goods.
  • ATM scams: Some fake ATMs offer to sell paysafecards. Only buy from staffed retail counters.
  • Lost PIN: If you lose a voucher that has not been scratched, the finder can use it. Treat vouchers like cash.

Lost or stolen vouchers: If your voucher is lost or stolen before you scratch the PIN, the retail receipt may help. Contact Paysafe support with the receipt details. Once the PIN is revealed, the voucher is as good as cash — protect it.

Account locking: If you suspect your myPaysafe account is compromised, log in immediately and lock it. Contact customer support to investigate any unauthorized transactions.

Recommendations by Use Case

  • Online shopping: PayPal (best buyer protection) or credit card directly
  • International money transfer: Wise (lowest fees, real exchange rate)
  • Gambling/gaming deposits: Paysafe (privacy) or Skrill/NETELLER (withdrawal support)
  • Travel spending: Revolut (multi-currency, good rates) or Wise card
  • Receiving freelance payments: Wise (multi-currency accounts) or PayPal (widely used)
  • Privacy/anonymous payments: Paysafe (cash purchase, no personal info)
  • Daily banking alternative: Revolut (budgeting, spending analytics, virtual cards)
