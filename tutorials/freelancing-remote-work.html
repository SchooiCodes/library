Freelancing & Remote Work

Freelance and remote work — finding clients, pricing, contracts, and productivity.

Table of Contents (2 sections)

  • Getting Started & Clients
  • Remote Work & Productivity

Getting Started & Clients

Freelance: self-employed, offer services to clients. Services: web dev, mobile apps, design, writing, marketing, consulting, technical writing. Platforms: Upwork (general), Toptal (high-end vetted), Fiverr (gig-based), Freelancer, Toptal, Contra (commission-free). Niche: specialize (WordPress expert, React Native dev) — command higher rates. Portfolio: examples of work, case studies, testimonials. Website: personal brand, services, portfolio, contact. Networking: LinkedIn (post content, engage), Twitter/X (share knowledge), local meetups, conferences. Cold outreach: identify target companies, personalized email, offer value. Rates: hourly ($50-200/hr) vs project-based (fixed price) vs value-based (based on value to client). Pricing factors: experience, location, specialization, demand. Negotiation: don't undersell, know your minimum, be willing to walk away. Retainers: ongoing monthly work — stable income. Contracts: scope of work, deliverables, timeline, payment terms, revisions limit, IP ownership, NDA. Invoicing: FreshBooks, Wave, Bonsai, PayPal, Wise, Stripe. Payment: milestone payments, upfront deposit (30-50%). Taxes: self-employment tax (US ~15.3%), quarterly estimated taxes, deductions (home office, equipment, software, internet).

Remote Work & Productivity

Remote work: work from anywhere. Types: fully remote (company), hybrid, remote-first (async). Async communication: write clearly, use documentation, over-communicate, record decisions. Tools: Slack/Discord (chat), Notion/Confluence (docs), Linear/Jira (tasks), Zoom/Google Meet (calls), Miro (whiteboard). Home office: dedicated space, ergonomic chair, desk, monitor, good lighting, noise control. Productivity: time blocking, deep work, no meeting days, context switching cost. Work-life balance: set boundaries (start/end time, not always on), separate workspace, morning routine. Burnout: overwork, isolation, lack of boundaries — take breaks, socialize, exercise. International: timezone overlap, cultural differences, travel (digital nomad). Visas: digital nomad visas (Portugal D7, Estonia, Croatia, Thailand SMART), tax residency. Health insurance: international coverage (SafetyWing, World Nomads, Cigna Global). Retirement: solo 401k, SEP IRA, IRA (US). Business structure: sole proprietor, LLC (US), Ltd (UK) — liability protection. Client management: expectations, status updates, scope creep prevention. Growth: raise rates yearly, get referrals, build passive income (products, courses). Community: freelancer groups (r/freelance, r/digitalnomad), co-working spaces, coliving. Failure: dry spells — save 3-6 months runway.
