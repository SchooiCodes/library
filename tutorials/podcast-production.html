Audio Production

Podcast Production Guide

Plan, record, edit, and publish a professional podcast — from equipment to distribution.

Table of Contents (5 sections)

  • Planning & Equipment
  • Recording Techniques
  • Editing Workflow
  • Music, ID3 Tags & Show Notes
  • Distribution & Growth

Planning & Equipment

Define your podcast concept: topic, format (solo/monologue, co-host interview, panel discussion), episode length, release schedule (weekly/bi-weekly), and target audience.

Equipment: microphone (USB: Blue Yeti, Samson Q2U, Rode NT-USB; XLR: Shure SM58, Audio-Technica ATR2100x), audio interface (Focusrite Scarlett, Behringer U-Phoria), headphones (closed-back for recording), pop filter, boom arm, and portable recorder (Zoom H5) for field recording.

Acoustic treatment: record in a closet full of clothes or use portable reflection filters. Minimize reverb by hanging blankets on walls. Software: Audacity (free), OBS (free), Reaper $60.

Recording Techniques

Recording checklist: check levels (-12dB to -6dB peaks), set sample rate (44100Hz or 48000Hz), bit depth (24-bit), and file format (WAV). Record each person on a separate track.

Mic technique: 4-6 inches from mouth, slightly off-axis to reduce plosives, consistent distance. Use a pop filter for plosive reduction. Monitor with closed-back headphones to prevent bleed.

Remote recording: use platforms like Riverside.fm, Zencastr, SquadCast, or record locally with each person on their own track and sync later. Always have a backup recording on your local device.

Editing Workflow

Post-production workflow: 1) Select best takes, 2) Remove long pauses and verbal fillers (um, uh, like), 3) Balance levels between speakers, 4) Add music intro/outro, 5) EQ and compression for clarity.

Vocal processing chain: Noise Gate → Noise Reduction (sample noise floor) → Compressor (3:1 ratio, gentle) → EQ (low cut below 80Hz, gentle presence boost at 3kHz) → Limiter (catch peaks).

Use spectral editing (Audacity's Spectrogram view) to visually remove clicks, pops, mouth noises, and background hum. Save editing templates/presets for consistent workflow.

Music, ID3 Tags & Show Notes

Intro/outro music: use royalty-free music from Free Music Archive, Incompetech, Pixabay Music, or Uppbeat. Fade in/out for smooth transitions. Duration: 15-30 seconds typical.

ID3 tags: Title, Artist (podcast name), Album (season/episode), Year, Genre (Podcast), Comments (episode description), and Artwork (3000x3000px JPEG/PNG). Use MP3Tag or similar.

Show notes (SEO crucial): episode summary, timestamps (key topics), guest bio (if applicable), links mentioned, transcript (accessibility + SEO), and calls to action (subscribe, rate, review).

Distribution & Growth

Hosting platforms: Buzzsprout, Transistor, Captivate, Podbean, or self-host with Castopod (free). RSS feed is the universal distribution mechanism.

Submit RSS to directories: Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Google Podcasts, Amazon Music, Castbox, Pocket Casts, Overcast, and Stitcher. Submit to each platform (takes 1-5 days for approval).

Growth: consistent schedule, cross-promotion with other podcasts, social media snippets (Audiograms with Wavve/Headliner), guest appearances, listener engagement (Q&A episodes), ratings/reviews.
