Storage (SSD, NVMe, HDD)

Storage technology — SSD, NVMe, HDD, RAID, and data reliability.

Table of Contents (2 sections)

  • SSD & NVMe
  • HDD & Reliability

SSD & NVMe

SSD: NAND flash storage — no moving parts, fast random access. NAND types: SLC (1 bit/cell — fastest, most durable), MLC (2 bits), TLC (3 bits — consumer standard), QLC (4 bits — cheaper, slower writes, lower endurance). 3D NAND: vertical stacking — 128-238+ layers. Form factors: 2.5-inch (SATA), M.2 2280 (NVMe or SATA), U.2 (enterprise), EDSFF (enterprise). SATA SSD: up to 550 MB/s (SATA 3 limit). NVMe: PCIe interface — up to 14 GB/s (PCIe 5.0 x4), 1M+ IOPS. Protocols: AHCI (legacy, SATA), NVMe (native PCIe — queue depth 65k vs AHCI 32). NVMe features: multiple queues (per-core), interrupt coalescing, streamlined command set. DRAM vs DRAM-less: DRAM has host memory buffer (HMB) uses system RAM for mapping table. Over-provisioning: spare NAND for wear leveling. TRIM: inform SSD of unused blocks. Endurance: TBW (terabytes written) rating.

HDD & Reliability

HDD: spinning magnetic platters, read/write head on arm. RPM: 5400 (low power), 7200 (desktop), 10k/15k (enterprise). Areal density: bits per square inch — increases yearly (CMR vs SMR). CMR (Conventional Magnetic Recording): standard tracks. SMR (Shingled Magnetic Recording): overlapping tracks — more capacity, slower random writes. Form factors: 3.5-inch (desktop), 2.5-inch (laptop). Interfaces: SATA (consumer), SAS (enterprise — dual-port, better error handling). Reliability: MTBF (mean time between failures — 1-2M hours), AFR (annualized failure rate ~1-3%). SMART: self-monitoring attributes — reallocated sectors, pending sectors, spin retry count. RAID levels: 0 (stripe — performance, no redundancy), 1 (mirror), 5 (striped parity — 1 drive failure), 6 (dual parity — 2 drives), 10 (stripe + mirror). RAID vs standalone: RAID provides redundancy/performance, not backup. Backup: 3-2-1 rule (3 copies, 2 media types, 1 off-site).
