Time Management

Manage time effectively — productivity systems, prioritization, and habit building.

Table of Contents (2 sections)

  • Productivity Systems
  • Habit Building & Focus

Productivity Systems

GTD (Getting Things Done — David Allen): capture (collect everything), clarify (what is it?), organize (projects, next actions, waiting, someday/maybe), reflect (weekly review), engage (do!). Kanban: visualize work — columns (To Do, In Progress, Done). Tools: Trello, Notion, Todoist, Linear. Eisenhower Matrix: urgent vs important — Do First (urgent+important), Schedule (important not urgent), Delegate (urgent not important), Eliminate (neither). Time blocking: schedule specific tasks in calendar blocks. Deep Work (Cal Newport): minimize shallow tasks, batch deep work. Parkinson's Law: work expands to fill time — set tight deadlines. Eat the Frog: do hardest task first. Two-minute rule: if task takes <2 min, do it now. Pomodoro: 25/5 or 50/10 for deep work. Warren Buffett's 25-5 list: pick top 5 priorities, avoid everything else. Weekly review: plan upcoming week, check progress on goals. Daily planning: 10 min each morning — top 3 priorities. Energy management: match high-energy tasks to peak hours. Batching: group similar tasks (emails, meetings, errands).

Habit Building & Focus

Atomic Habits (James Clear): 1% better each day. Four laws: 1) Make it obvious (cue), 2) Make it attractive (craving), 3) Make it easy (response), 4) Make it satisfying (reward). Habit stacking: after [current habit], I will [new habit]. Environment design: arrange space for desired habits. Cue: habit trigger — time, location, preceding event. Routine: the behavior. Reward: dopamine release. Tracking: habit tracker (Don't Break the Chain). Streaks: consecutive days — motivate consistency. Accountability partner: share goals, check progress. Temptation bundling: pair pleasurable activity with needed task. Focus blockers: social media (app blockers, site blockers), phone (greyscale, Do Not Disturb). Single-tasking: one task at a time — avoid multitasking (reduces productivity 40%). Attention residue: switching costs between tasks. State-based tasking: match task to energy level (creative in morning, admin in afternoon). Calendar blocking: protect deep work time. Make time for: breaks, exercise, sleep, relationships. Reflection: journal weekly — what worked, what to improve. Decision fatigue: reduce trivial decisions (meal prep, routine clothes). 80/20 rule (Pareto): 80% of results from 20% of efforts.
