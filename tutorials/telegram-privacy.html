Telegram Privacy Guide

A comprehensive walkthrough of every Telegram privacy setting to lock down your account and protect your identity.

Overview

Telegram is often praised for its speed and features, but its default privacy settings leave a lot exposed. Unlike Signal or WhatsApp, Telegram does not enable end-to-end encryption by default for regular chats. Only "Secret Chats" use E2EE, and they are limited to one-on-one conversations on the same device. This guide walks through every privacy lever Telegram offers so you can decide what level of exposure you are comfortable with.

Important: Cloud chats (the default) are encrypted in transit and at rest on Telegram's servers, but Telegram holds the encryption keys. If you need true end-to-end encryption, always use Secret Chats or a different app entirely.

Secret Chats vs Cloud Chats

Understanding the difference is the most important concept in Telegram privacy.

Feature Cloud Chat Secret Chat
End-to-End EncryptedNoYes
Synced across devicesYesNo (device only)
Self-Destruct TimerNoYes
Forwarding ProtectionLimitedFull (cannot forward)
Server stores messagesYesNo

To start a Secret Chat, open a user's profile → More → Start Secret Chat. You cannot downgrade an existing chat; it creates a new conversation.

Note: Secret Chats are device-specific. If you delete the app or lose the device, the history is gone permanently. They also do not support groups.

Privacy Settings

Navigate to Settings → Privacy and Security. This is the control center for your Telegram privacy.

Phone Number

By default your phone number is visible to everyone. Change it:

  • Who can see my phone number: Set to Nobody or My Contacts
  • Who can find me by my number: Set to My Contacts or Nobody

If you set phone number visibility to Nobody, Telegram will still show it to people who already have it saved in their address book. To prevent this entirely, you need to use a virtual number or register with a burner SIM.

Last Seen & Online

Options: Everybody, My Contacts, Nobody, or Except...

Set to Nobody to hide your online status completely. Note: if you set this to Nobody, you also will not be able to see the last seen status of others — Telegram enforces reciprocity.

Profile Photo

Control who can see your profile photo: Everybody, My Contacts, Nobody, or custom exceptions. Setting this to My Contacts is a good balance if you want people you know to recognize you.

Forwarded Messages

When someone forwards a message from you, it can display a link back to your account. Change this to Nobody to prevent your account from being linked. Messages forwarded from you will still show your name but will not include a clickable link.

Two-Step Verification

Two-step verification (2FA) adds a password on top of the SMS code. This prevents anyone who has your SIM from accessing your account.

  1. Go to Settings → Privacy and Security → Two-Step Verification
  2. Tap Set Password
  3. Enter a strong password (not your SMS code)
  4. Add a password hint (optional but helpful)
  5. Provide a recovery email — this is critical if you forget your password
Warning: If you lose your 2FA password AND cannot access your recovery email, you will be permanently locked out of your Telegram account with no recovery option. Save your recovery email access securely.

To set up a recovery email that Telegram supports:

  • Use a dedicated email account with its own 2FA
  • Do not reuse this email across random services
  • Example: telegram-recovery@protonmail.com

Passcode Lock

A passcode locks the Telegram app on your device. This is separate from your 2FA password.

  1. Settings → Privacy and Security → Passcode Lock
  2. Enable and set a 4-digit code or a custom alphanumeric password
  3. Optionally enable Auto-Lock after 1/5/15 minutes or immediately
  4. Toggle Lock in Background to lock when switching apps

On Android, you can also enable Fingerprint Unlock for convenience. On iOS, Face ID / Touch ID is supported.

Active Sessions

Telegram keeps you logged in across all devices. Review and terminate sessions regularly.

  1. Settings → Privacy and Security → Active Sessions
  2. You will see a list of all currently active sessions with device model, OS, location (approx), and IP address
  3. Tap the three dots next to any session to terminate it remotely
  4. Tap Terminate All Other Sessions to log out everywhere except your current device

A best practice is to review this list monthly. If you see a session you do not recognize, terminate it immediately and change your 2FA password.

Bot Privacy

Bots are small programs that can interact with your chats. They operate under strict limitations if you configure your privacy correctly.

  • Block bots by default: Do not start conversations with untrusted bots
  • Bot privacy in groups: By default, a bot added to a group can see all messages. Change this in Settings → Privacy and Security → Groups → Who can add me to groups? — set to My Contacts to prevent random bot groups
  • Never share your phone number with a bot: Bots do not see your phone number unless you explicitly share it via a command like /phone
Caution: Bots can see your IP address if you click on links they send. Use a proxy (see below) to mask your IP if you frequently interact with untrusted bots.

Proxy Settings

Telegram supports SOCKS5 and MTProto proxies to mask your IP address from the server. This is useful in countries that block Telegram or simply to hide your location.

  1. Settings → Data and Storage → Proxy Settings
  2. Tap Add Proxy and choose SOCKS5 or MTProto
  3. Enter the proxy hostname, port, and optionally username/password
  4. Toggle Use Proxy to enable

Example SOCKS5 proxy configuration:

Host: 127.0.0.1
Port: 9050
Username: (leave blank)
Password: (leave blank)
Type: SOCKS5

This setup routes Telegram through a local Tor SOCKS proxy. For MTProto, you will need a proxy server that supports the protocol — free proxies can be found via bot listings but verify their legitimacy before use.

Groups & Channels Privacy

Telegram groups and channels can expose you in several ways. Manage these settings carefully.

Who can add me to groups?

Settings → Privacy and Security → Groups
Set to My Contacts or Nobody. If you set it to My Contacts, people not in your contacts will receive a request that you must approve. Set to Nobody to prevent anyone from adding you without your explicit approval link.

Channel Privacy

When you join a channel:

  • Public channels — anyone can see the subscriber list. Your profile is visible to channel admins.
  • Private channels — invite-only. Your profile is still visible to admins but not to other subscribers unless you interact.

To comment anonymously in channels, consider using a different Telegram account or a pseudonym if the channel allows it.

Who can call me?

Settings → Privacy and Security → Calls
Set to My Contacts or Nobody to prevent unwanted calls. You can also enable Peer-to-Peer calls which hide your IP but may degrade call quality.

Deleting Your Account

If you decide to leave Telegram, you can delete your account permanently. This is irreversible.

  1. Settings → Privacy and Security → Delete My Account
  2. Enter your phone number and confirmation code sent via SMS
  3. Select Delete My Account

All messages, contacts, groups, and channels are permanently removed. This can take up to 30 days to fully process on Telegram's servers.

Self-Destruct Timer

You can also set an inactivity self-destruct timer:

Settings → Privacy and Security → If I'm away for
Choose from 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, or 1 year. If you do not log in during that period, your account is automatically deleted. This is useful if you fear losing access to your phone number.

Tip: Set the self-destruct timer to the maximum (1 year) to avoid accidental deletion if you travel and cannot access Telegram for a while.
