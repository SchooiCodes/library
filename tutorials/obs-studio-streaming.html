OBS Studio Streaming

Professional live streaming, recording, and video production with OBS Studio — the free streaming software.

OBS Setup & Configuration

OBS Studio (Open Broadcaster Software) is free, open-source software for video recording and live streaming. Download from obsproject.com for Windows, macOS, or Linux.

First-time setup: Auto-Configuration Wizard detects your system and recommends optimal settings. Choose 'Optimize for streaming' or 'Optimize for recording' based on your use case.

Key settings: Output (bitrate, encoder, resolution), Video (base/resolution, FPS, downscale filter), Audio (sample rate, desktop audio device, mic device), and Advanced (color format, color space).

Scenes & Sources

Scenes are collections of sources. Organize your stream with multiple scenes: 'Starting Soon', 'Live', 'Be Right Back', 'Ending', and 'Gameplay'. Switch between them during the stream.

Source types: Display Capture (full screen), Window Capture (specific app), Game Capture (DX/OpenGL), Video Capture Device (webcam), Image, Text (GDI+), Media Source, Browser Source (overlays, alerts), and Color Source (backgrounds).

Sources stack like layers. Use filters: color correction, chroma key (green screen), crop/pad, noise suppression, gain, compressor, and limiter for audio.

Audio Engineering in OBS

Professional audio matters as much as video quality. OBS supports per-source audio filters.

Essential audio filters: Noise Gate (cuts silence), Noise Suppression (removes background hiss), Compressor (evens volume), Limiter (prevents clipping), Gain (volume boost), and Equalizer (tone shaping).

Audio monitoring: Monitor and Output (hear and stream), Monitor Only (hear but don't stream), and Off (mute). Use VST plugins for advanced effects like reverb, delay, and parametric EQ.

Streaming to Platforms

Stream to Twitch, YouTube, or Facebook Live by connecting your stream key. Settings → Stream → Service → select platform → paste stream key (from platform's dashboard).

NEVER share your stream key — anyone with it can stream as you. Use OBS's 'Reset Stream Key' if it's compromised. For multi-streaming, use services like Restream.io.

Configure stream settings: bitrate (4500-6000 Kbps for 1080p), encoder (NVENC/AMF for hardware, x264 for software), keyframe interval (2 seconds), and preset (quality vs performance trade-off).

Advanced: Replays, Transitions & Plugins

Replay Buffer captures the last N seconds of gameplay — perfect for saving highlights without recording the entire session. Enable in Settings → Output → Replay Buffer.

Transitions: Cut, Fade, Stinger (video file with audio), Slide, and Swipe. Add scene transition overrides for specific scene pairs. Stinger transitions require a video file with alpha channel (webm with transparency).

Plugins extend OBS: StreamFX (3D effects, shaders, motion blur), Move (animation), Source Record (record individual sources), and NDIV (wireless source sharing across network).
