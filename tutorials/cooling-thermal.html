Cooling & Thermal

PC cooling — air, AIO, custom loop, thermal paste, and fan optimization.

Table of Contents (2 sections)

  • Air vs Liquid Cooling

Air vs Liquid Cooling

Air cooling: heatsink + fan — heat pipes (copper, heat-pipe direct touch vs nickel-plated), fin density, tower height. Types: low-profile (ITX), single tower (mid-range CPUs), dual tower (high-end — Noctua NH-D15, Thermalright Peerless Assassin). Fans: size (120mm, 140mm), bearing (sleeve, hydraulic, FDB — fluid dynamic bearing, rifle), static pressure (radiators/radiators + fine fins), airflow (case ventilation), PWM (4-pin — variable speed) vs DC (3-pin). AIO (All-in-One): pump, radiator, fans, pre-filled. Sizes: 120mm, 240mm, 280mm, 360mm, 420mm. Performance: 280mm ~= 360mm (140mm fans run quieter). Thickness: 27mm standard, 45mm+ high-performance (push-pull fans). Custom loop: pump/reservoir, CPU block, GPU block, radiator, fittings, tubing (soft/PETG/acrylic), coolant (distilled + biocide or premix). Maintenance: drain, refill yearly. Materials: copper (good) vs aluminum (cheaper) — never mix (galvanic corrosion).

Thermal Management

Thermal paste: TIM (thermal interface material) — fills microscopic gaps between CPU and cooler. Types: thermal grease (NT-H1, MX-6 — easy), liquid metal (Conductonaut — best conductivity but conductive, risky), thermal pads (for GPUs, VRAM). Application: pea method (center), line method (Intel LGA), spread method (thin layer). Thermal paste lifespan: 2-5 years before drying out. Thermal throttling: CPU/GPU reduce frequency when temperature exceeds threshold (~100°C CPU, ~85°C GPU). Junction temp (Tjmax): hottest spot on die. Ambient temperature: affects cooling performance. Case airflow: front intake, rear/top exhaust — positive pressure (more intake than exhaust — less dust). Fan curves: BIOS or software (FanControl, SpeedFan) — define temperature:rpm mapping. Water cooling temp: target 35-40°C coolant under load. Radiator placement: top (exhaust) or front (intake) — push vs pull vs push-pull. Fan control: PWM splitter, fan hub, Aquacomputer Quadro/Octo. Thermal pads: for VRAM, VRM (replace if too thick/thin). Undervolting: reduce voltage without losing stability — lower temps. Power limits: PL1 (sustained) and PL2 (boost) for Intel CPUs.
