Offline Maps & Navigation

Download offline maps for navigation when there's no data connection.

Table of Contents (3 sections)

  • Offline Map Apps
  • Download Strategy
  • Satellite & Topographic

Offline Map Apps

Organic Maps: open-source offline maps based on OpenStreetMap data — walking, driving, cycling, public transit navigation. Download entire countries, no internet needed for routing. Maps.me: popular alternative with hotel/restaurant listings. OsmAnd: most feature-rich OSM app — contour lines, hiking trails, GPX track recording. All three: download maps per-country, offline search, offline turn-by-turn navigation.

Download Strategy

Download maps before travel or while on Wi-Fi. Strategy: home country (full detail), neighboring countries (road networks), destinations (city detail). Map sizes: USA ~5GB (full), Europe ~10GB (full), state/province ~200MB-1GB. Storage: download to SD card on Android (256GB+ recommended for global coverage). Update every 3-6 months (OpenStreetMap is updated continuously). Enable automatic updates on Wi-Fi in app settings.

Satellite & Topographic

Satellite imagery offline: Google Maps allows caching 30mi² per download, but requires periodic refresh. Organic Maps: no satellite imagery but has detailed terrain. Topographic maps: OsmAnd with contour lines plugin — elevation data for hiking, off-road navigation. GPS without data: all offline maps use the device's GPS chip (works without internet). Download GPX tracks for specific hikes/routes from AllTrails or Komoot before going offline.
