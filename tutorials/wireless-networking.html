Wireless networks — WiFi 6/7, mesh, security, and spectrum management.

WiFi Standards & Spectrum

WiFi standards: 802.11b (11 Mbps, 2.4 GHz), 802.11g (54 Mbps, 2.4 GHz), 802.11n (WiFi 4 — up to 600 Mbps, MIMO), 802.11ac (WiFi 5 — up to 3.5 Gbps, 5 GHz, MU-MIMO), 802.11ax (WiFi 6 — up to 9.6 Gbps, 2.4/5/6 GHz, OFDMA, TWT), 802.11be (WiFi 7 — up to 46 Gbps, 320 MHz channels, multi-link operation). Frequency bands: 2.4 GHz (longer range, more interference, 3 non-overlapping channels 1/6/11), 5 GHz (shorter range, more channels, DFS), 6 GHz (WiFi 6E/7 — very clean, 1200 MHz spectrum). MIMO: multiple antennas for spatial streams. MU-MIMO: simultaneous transmission to multiple devices. Beamforming: focus signal toward clients.

Security & Mesh

Wireless security: WPA3 (SAE handshake — dictionary attack resistant, 192-bit mode), WPA2 (CCMP/AES, pre-shared key — vulnerable to KRACK). WPA3-Enterprise: 802.1X with EAP-TLS (certificate auth). RADIUS: centralized auth — FreeRADIUS, Cisco ISE. EAP methods: PEAP, EAP-TLS, EAP-TTLS. Mesh networks: multiple APs with one SSID — seamless roaming (802.11r/k/v fast roaming). Mesh vs wired AP: mesh easier to deploy, wired AP better performance. Captive portal: splash page for guest access. WiFi analytics: signal strength (RSSI), channel utilization, interference sources. RF planning: site survey (Ekahau, NetSpot), heat maps, channel reuse. PoE: power over Ethernet (802.3af 15.4W, 802.3at 30W, 802.3bt 60/90W).
