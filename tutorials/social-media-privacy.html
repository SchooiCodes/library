Overview

Social media platforms are designed to collect as much data about you as possible. Every like, share, comment, and even the time you spend viewing a post feeds into profiling algorithms. This guide provides a platform-by-platform checklist to lock down your privacy settings, limit data sharing, and remove old content. It also covers privacy-focused alternatives if you are ready to leave the walled gardens.

Facebook Privacy

Facebook's settings are notoriously complex and buried. Follow these steps to minimize exposure.

Essential Settings

  1. Profile & Tagging → Who can post on your profile? Set to Only Me
  2. Profile & Tagging → Review tags people add to your posts before they appear: Enable
  3. Public Posts → Who can follow me? Set to Friends
  4. Public Posts → Public Post Comments: Set to Friends
  5. Timeline & Tagging → Who can see posts you're tagged in on your profile? Set to Only Me

Privacy Shortcuts

  • Settings → Privacy → Your Activity: Review posts and things you are tagged in
  • Settings → Face Recognition: Set to Off
  • Settings → Ads → Ad Preferences: Turn off ads based on activity with partners and advertisers
  • Settings → Location: Disable location history and check-in settings
Warning: Facebook's "Off-Facebook Activity" lets them track you across the web even if you do not have a Facebook account. Go to Settings → Your Facebook Information → Off-Facebook Activity → Clear History to disconnect this. You must also disable future activity.

Download Your Data

Before making changes, download your Facebook data as a record of what they hold:

Settings → Your Facebook Information → Download Your Information
Format: JSON (more complete) or HTML (readable)
Media quality: Low (unless you want full-resolution photos)
Deselect unnecessary categories (e.g., ads data, cookies)

Instagram Privacy

Instagram is owned by Meta and shares data with Facebook. Privacy controls are more limited but still worth adjusting.

Settings Checklist

  1. Private Account: Enable under Settings → Privacy → Account Privacy. This prevents strangers from seeing your posts and stories.
  2. Activity Status: Disable under Settings → Privacy → Activity Status
  3. Story Controls: Set Settings → Privacy → Story → Hide Story From to exclude specific people
  4. Close Friends: Use Close Friends lists for sensitive stories instead of posting to all followers
  5. Tagged Posts: Enable Manually Approve Tags under Settings → Privacy → Tags
  6. Mentions: Set to People You Follow under Settings → Privacy → Mentions

Data Sharing

  • Settings → Account → Sharing to Other Apps → Stories: Disable auto-sharing to Facebook
  • Settings → Account → Contacts Syncing: Disable to prevent Instagram from uploading your contacts
  • Settings → Security → Login Activity: Review active sessions regularly

Twitter/X Privacy

Twitter's privacy settings changed significantly after the platform rebranded to X. Here is how to lock down your account.

Privacy & Safety

  1. Settings → Privacy and Safety → Protect your posts: Enable (makes your account private, all posts require approval to follow)
  2. Photo Tagging → Anyone can tag you in photos: Set to No one
  3. Discoverability → Let others find you by your email/phone: Disable both

Data Sharing & Personalization

  • Settings → Privacy and Safety → Off-Twitter Activity → Allow data sharing with business partners: Disable
  • Settings → Privacy and Safety → Personalization and Data: Disable all toggles (Personalized ads, Personalized trends, Location history)
  • Settings → Privacy and Safety → Direct Messages → Allow message requests from everyone: Set to Only people you follow

Remove Old Tweets

Use third-party tools to bulk delete old tweets (use with caution):

# Use "TweetDelete" (web tool) or "Twitter Archive Eraser"
# Download your archive first: Settings → Your Account → Download an archive of your data
# Then use a deletion tool to remove tweets older than N days

# For power users: use the Twitter API with Python
# pip install tweepy
# https://github.com/tweepy/tweepy
Caution: Third-party tweet deletion tools require API access. Revoke their permissions once you are done. For maximum privacy, simply delete your account and start fresh.

TikTok Privacy

TikTok has faced significant scrutiny over data collection practices. Its privacy settings offer more control than many people realize.

Essential Settings

  1. Settings → Privacy → Private Account: Enable (default is public)
  2. Settings → Privacy → Direct Messages → Who can send you messages: Set to Friends or No one
  3. Settings → Privacy → Liked Videos → Like count and liked videos visible to others: Disable
  4. Settings → Privacy → Comments → Who can comment on your videos: Set to Friends or No one
  5. Settings → Privacy → Duet and Stitch: Set to Only me or Friends

Data Controls

  • Settings → Privacy → Personalization and data → Personalized ads: Disable
  • Settings → Privacy → Personalization and data → Download your data: Request a copy to see what TikTok has collected
  • Settings → Privacy → Account → Sync contacts: Disable
  • Settings → Privacy → Safety → Restricted Mode: Optionally enable to filter content
Data Concerns: TikTok's parent company ByteDance is subject to Chinese law. If you are concerned about data going to Chinese servers, consider not using TikTok at all. The app collects device info, browsing data, and keyboard input patterns.

Reddit Privacy

Reddit is largely anonymous by design, but your posting history can be used to identify you.

Privacy Settings

  1. User Settings → Profile → Show up in search results: Disable
  2. User Settings → Profile → Show active communities: Disable
  3. User Settings → Profile → Allow people to follow you: Disable
  4. User Settings → Safety & Privacy → Personalize ads based on activity on Reddit: Disable
  5. User Settings → Safety & Privacy → Personalize recommendations based on your activity on other sites: Disable

Anonymous Posting

  • Use temporary accounts (throwaways) for sensitive topics
  • Do not use the same username across Reddit and other platforms
  • Avoid posting personally identifiable information — even indirectly
  • Delete old posts periodically using tools like Reddit's own deletion or third-party scripts
# Using Reddit API to delete all comments (requires API key)
# pip install praw
# https://github.com/your-username/reddit-cleanup

# Manual deletion is tedious. Consider:
# - sh.reddit.com → Profile → Overview → Delete individual posts
# - Use old.reddit.com for bulk deletion in subreddit view
Note: Deleted Reddit comments can still be accessed through third-party archives like Reveddit, removeddit, or Pushshift. Even after deletion, assume your posts are permanently recorded somewhere.

App Permissions

Social media apps request extensive permissions on your phone. Review and revoke what they do not need.

Common Over-Permissions

Permission Why It Is Risky Action
ContactsUploads your entire address bookDeny
Location (always)Tracks your movements continuouslyDeny or "While Using App"
MicrophoneCan record ambient audio (TikTok, Instagram)Deny, only allow for recordings
CameraCan take photos/video without indicator"Ask Every Time" (iOS) or deny
Photos/MediaAccess to entire photo libraryLimited access or deny
BluetoothUsed for proximity trackingDeny

Revoking App Permissions

# Android: Settings → Apps → [App] → Permissions → Revoke
# iOS: Settings → Privacy → [Permission] → Toggle off [App]
# Facebook: Settings → Apps & Websites → Remove
# Google: myaccount.google.com → Security → Third-party apps with account access
# Twitter/X: Settings → Security → Connected Apps → Revoke

Getting Started with Mastodon

  1. Choose an instance — joinmastodon.org has a directory. Pick one aligned with your interests (e.g., mastodon.social, tech.lgbt)
  2. Create an account — requires email only, no phone number
  3. Set profile to private — Settings → Profile → Require follow requests
  4. Enable two-factor auth — Settings → Account → Two-factor Auth
  5. Control discoverability: Disable "Suggest my account to others" and "Show my account in search results"
  6. Use apps like Tusky (Android), Ice Cubes (iOS), or Elk (web) for a better UX
Why Federated? ActivityPub-based platforms do not have a central company controlling your data. You choose an instance, and you can migrate your account to another instance. No algorithm manipulates your timeline.
