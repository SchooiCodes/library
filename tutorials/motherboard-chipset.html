Motherboard & Chipset

Motherboard — chipsets, PCIe lanes, VRM, form factors, and BIOS/UEFI.

Table of Contents (2 sections)

  • Chipsets & PCIe
  • VRM & BIOS/UEFI

Chipsets & PCIe

Chipset: connects CPU to peripherals (SATA, USB, LAN, audio, secondary M.2). Intel chipsets: B760 (mid-range, PCIe 4.0), Z790 (high-end, overclocking, PCIe 5.0 GPU + 4.0 storage), Z890 (Arrow Lake — new LGA1851, Thunderbolt 4). AMD chipsets: B650 (mid-range, PCIe 5.0 GPU + storage), X670E (high-end, dual chipset, PCIe 5.0), X870E (new — USB4, PCIe 5.0). PCIe lanes: direct from CPU (x16 GPU, x4 M.2) and from chipset (additional M.2, USB, SATA, Ethernet). Gen 4: 16 GT/s per lane (~2 GB/s). Gen 5: 32 GT/s (~4 GB/s). Gen 6: 64 GT/s (~8 GB/s). PCIe bifurcation: split x16 into x8/x8 (dual GPU) or x8/x4/x4 (GPU + 2 SSDs). M.2 slots: CPU-attached (direct, faster) vs chipset-attached (shared bandwidth). SATA: 6 Gbps per port. USB: controller in chipset — USB 3.2 Gen2x2 (20 Gbps), USB4/TB4 (40 Gbps).

VRM & BIOS/UEFI

VRM (Voltage Regulator Module): converts 12V to CPU/IMC voltage — phases (doublers), MOSFETs, chokes, capacitors. Phase count: more phases = cleaner power, less ripple. Quality: low RDS(on) MOSFETs, high-quality capacitors (Japanese). Power stages (DrMOS): integrated MOSFET + driver — more efficient. BIOS/UEFI: firmware interface — UEFI (modern, GPT, Secure Boot, GUI) vs legacy BIOS (MBR). Settings: XMP/EXPO (RAM overclock), PBO (AMD Precision Boost Overdrive — auto OC), undervolting (offset voltage), fan curves (PWM vs DC), boot order (UEFI vs CSM/Legacy), Resizable BAR/SAM (GPU memory access). Update: flash via USB (BIOS Flashback — no CPU needed). CMOS reset: remove battery or jumper. POST codes: debug LED, beep codes, Q-Code display. Form factors: ATX (305x244mm), mATX (244x244mm), ITX (170x170mm), E-ATX (305x330mm).
