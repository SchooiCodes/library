Esports & Competitive Gaming

Competitive gaming — practice routines, aim training, team strategy, and mental game.

Table of Contents (2 sections)

  • Practice & Aim Training

Practice & Aim Training

Deliberate practice: focused improvement on weaknesses, not just playing. Aim trainers: Kovaak's, Aim Lab (free), Aim Beast — scenarios (click timing, tracking, target switching). Daily routine: 15-30min aim training before play. Warm-up: deathmatch or practice range (10-15 min). Crosshair placement: always at head height, pre-aim common angles. Movement: counter-strafing (stop instantly for accurate shot), bunny hopping, slide jumping, peeker's advantage. Game sense: map knowledge, spawn timers, economy management, ult tracking, minimap awareness. VOD review: watch own replays — identify mistakes (positioning, crosshair placement, decision making). Demo review: pros' POV — study positioning, utility usage, rotations. Communication: clear callouts (enemy, damage, utility), concise, no clutter. Role mastery: main one role/agent/champion — master mechanics + macro decisions. Replay analysis tools: recording (OBS), Nvidia ShadowPlay, in-game demos, Outplayed. Kovaak's benchmarks: Volt9, Air PTS, Pasu Track, Smoothbot. Mechanics vs game sense: mechanics win fights, game sense wins matches.

Team Strategy & Mental

Team composition: roles/synergies — entry fragger, support, IGL (in-game leader), AWPer/sniper, flex. Economy management: team buy rounds (full buy, force buy, eco/save). Utility usage: smokes (vision block), flashes (blind enemies), mollies (area denial), grenades (damage). Default: spread out, gather info, play time. Execute: coordinated push on site — utility dump + entry. Retake: reclaim site after plant — clear angles, trade, default. Team practice: scrims, demo review, set plays, utility lineups. Mental game: tilt management — take breaks, breathing exercises, growth mindset. Consistency: regular sleep, hydration, exercise, ergonomic setup (chair, desk, monitor height). Burnout: vary practice, take rest days, balance gaming with other activities. Tournament prep: meta analysis, anti-strats for opponents, veto preparation. LAN vs online: LAN has no ping, crowd, different pressure. Clutch mentality: stay calm, isolate 1v1s, use utility, play time. Post-match review: win/loss, what worked, what to improve. Coaching: structured sessions, VOD review focus, drills. Analytics: stats tracking (HLTV, OP.GG, Dak.gg), performance trends.
