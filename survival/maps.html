How Offline Maps Work

Offline map apps pre-download map tiles and vector data to your device so you can navigate without any internet connection. GPS is a separate system — your phone's GPS receiver works entirely offline by listening to satellites.

  • Vector maps — Store map data as geometry (roads, buildings, coastlines) rather than images. Much smaller file sizes, supports dynamic styling and search.
  • Raster tiles — Pre-rendered image tiles (like Google Maps screenshots). Larger files, but look exactly like the online version. Common in Google Maps offline mode.
  • OpenStreetMap (OSM) — The data source behind most offline map apps. A free, editable map of the entire world, built by volunteers. Organic Maps, Maps.me, and OsmAnd all use OSM data.
  • Map rendering — The app draws the map on screen from vector data. Styles can be customized (day/night, terrain, cycle paths, etc.).
Key fact: GPS is free and always on. The US government made GPS publicly available in 1983 after a Korean Air Lines tragedy. GLONASS (Russia), Galileo (EU), and BeiDou (China) provide additional satellite coverage for faster locks and better accuracy.

Organic Maps

Organic Maps is a fully open-source, privacy-focused offline map app. No ads, no tracking, no account required. Uses OpenStreetMap data with beautiful, clean rendering.

  • Platform: Android, iOS (also available on F-Droid)
  • Features: Turn-by-turn navigation for driving, cycling, walking; offline search; bookmarks; GPX track import/export; contour lines for hiking
  • Map downloads: Entire countries or regions within countries. Incremental updates.
  • Size: Full planet ~50 GB, typical country ~100 MB–2 GB
  • Privacy: No data collection at all. Everything stays on your device.

# Android: F-Droid or Google Play

# Download maps via the in-app downloader

# /storage/emulated/0/Android/data/app.organicmaps/files/bookmarks/
Why Organic Maps? It's fully open-source (GitHub), uses minimal battery, works entirely offline, and has no trackers. It's the ethical choice for offline navigation.

Maps.me

Maps.me is one of the most popular offline map apps, with over 100 million downloads. Fast, reliable, and easy to use. Based on OpenStreetMap data with community editing.

  • Platform: Android, iOS
  • Features: Turn-by-turn navigation, offline search, public transit routes, hiking trails, bookmark syncing (optional account), hotel and restaurant listings from OSM
  • Map downloads: Countries, regions, and cities. Downloaded maps are stored on your device's SD card or internal storage.
  • Size: Similar to Organic Maps (they share a common heritage — Maps.me was originally based on the same engine before Organic Maps forked).

Maps.me has shifted toward a more commercial model in recent years with in-app purchases and hotel booking partnerships. The core offline mapping is still free and excellent.

OsmAnd

OsmAnd (OSM Automated Navigation Directions) is the most feature-rich offline map app available. It's aimed at power users, cyclists, hikers, and anyone who needs detailed control over their maps.

  • Platform: Android, iOS (Android version is more mature)
  • Features: Turn-by-turn voice navigation, offline POI search, public transit, GPX recording and navigation, contour lines and hillshade (add-on), nautical charts, audio/voice notes, speed cameras, lane guidance, truck routes
  • Map downloads: Countries, regions, or individual OSM tiles. Also supports online tile sources (satellite imagery, custom tile servers).
  • Versions: Free (limited to 10 map downloads) and Paid+ (unlimited downloads, contour lines, trip recording)

Google Maps Offline Mode

Yes, Google Maps has offline capabilities. It's not as full-featured as dedicated offline apps, but it's already installed on most phones.

  • How it works: Download a rectangular area of the map (raster tiles) for offline use. Max ~6 GB per area.
  • Limitations: No transit directions offline, no lane guidance, no offline search by name (can only search saved places). Maps expire after 15 days unless refreshed.
  • When to use: Short-term trips, city visits, when you already use Google Maps and don't want a second app.
# Download offline map in Google Maps
# 1. Open Google Maps
# 2. Tap your profile picture → Offline maps
# 3. Tap "Select your own map"
# 4. Drag the selection rectangle over the area you need
# 5. Tap "Download"

# Auto-update offline maps:
# Settings → Offline maps → Auto-update offline maps
# Choose: "Auto-update over Wi-Fi only" or "Never"

Important: Google Maps offline maps expire. You must refresh them at least once every 15 days with an internet connection. For long-term offline use, use Organic Maps or OsmAnd instead.

GPS Without Internet

Your phone's GPS chip is completely independent of internet connectivity. Here's how it works and how to maximize its effectiveness in offline scenarios:

  • A-GPS vs. standalone GPS: A-GPS (Assisted GPS) uses cellular towers or Wi-Fi to speed up the initial satellite lock. Without it, standalone GPS takes 30-60 seconds to get a first fix. Once locked, accuracy is ~3-5 meters either way.
  • GNSS constellations: Modern phones support GPS (US) + GLONASS (Russia) + Galileo (EU) + BeiDou (China). More satellites = faster lock + better accuracy. Enable all of them in your phone's location settings.
  • GPS apps that work offline: GPS Test (Android), GPX Viewer, Locus Maps. Use these to check satellite status and accuracy.
  • External GPS receivers: Bluetooth GPS receivers (e.g., Garmin GLO 2, Dual XGPS160) provide better accuracy and work with tablets that lack built-in GPS.
# Check GPS status offline (Android)
# Install "GPS Test" from F-Droid or Play Store
# Open the app outdoors with clear sky view
# Look for:
#  - Number of satellites in view (typically 12-20)
#  - Number of satellites used (at least 4 for 3D fix)
#  - Accuracy: 3-5 meters is normal
#  - Signal strength bars (green = good)

# Force GPS re-download of almanac data:
# Settings → Location → Scanning → Improve accuracy
# Or use the GPS Test app's "Reset GPS" feature
Critical: Download maps BEFORE you need them. Don't wait until you're in a remote area with no signal. A 64GB SD card can hold maps for most of a continent.

Creating and Sharing Custom Maps

Beyond app downloads, you can create your own offline maps from OpenStreetMap data or other sources:

  • OpenStreetMap export — Export any region as .osm, then convert to app-specific formats using offline converters.
  • GPS Visualizer — Create custom maps with overlays, waypoints, and tracks. Export as GPX for import into any map app.
  • QGIS — Professional GIS tool. Create map books, print atlases, and export georeferenced PDFs for offline use on any device.
  • Mobac (Mobile Atlas Creator) — Create offline atlases from various online map sources (Google, OSM, Bing, etc.). Export as OSMand, RMaps, or SQLite formats.
# Using Mobac to create an offline atlas
# 1. Download Mobac: https://mobac.sourceforge.io/
# 2. Select map source (e.g., OpenStreetMap)
# 3. Select area (draw rectangle or select by coordinates)
# 4. Select zoom levels (1-18, higher = more detail)
# 5. Select output format (OsmAnd SQLite, RMaps, MBTiles)
# 6. Generate atlas → transfer to phone SD card

# Convert OSM data to GPX for emergency navigation:
# wget -O map.osm "https://api.openstreetmap.org/api/0.6/map?bbox=-122.5,37.7,-122.3,37.8"
# Use osm2gpx or QGIS to convert and add waypoints

Emergency Navigation Preparedness

When your phone dies, you lose cell service, or GPS is unavailable, fall back to these methods:

  • Download maps before you go — This is the #1 rule. Before any trip to a remote area, download offline maps for the entire region plus surrounding areas.
  • Carry a backup device — A cheap Android phone with offline maps installed is better than nothing. Keep it charged and in your emergency kit.
  • Print physical maps — For critical navigation, paper maps don't need batteries. Mark key locations (water sources, shelters, exits, meeting points).
  • Compass and GPS coordinates — Know how to read a compass and grid references. Your phone's GPS Test app can show your current UTM or lat/lon coordinates even offline.
  • Pre-download points of interest — OsmAnd and Organic Maps support offline POI search. Download Wikipedia POI data for your region — it adds descriptions and photos to landmarks, usable offline.
# Emergency offline waypoint file (save as emergency.gpx)
# 
# 
#  
#   Home
#   Home coordinates - memorize this!
#  
#  
#   Meeting Point
#   Emergency meeting location
#  
# 

# Share this file with family members
# Import into Organic Maps or OsmAnd before departure
Important: Battery dies fast with GPS active. Your phone's GPS + screen-on can drain a full battery in 4-6 hours. Carry a power bank, use airplane mode, and limit screen-on time. OsmAnd and Organic Maps have power-saving modes.

Detailed Map Layers

Organic Maps supports multiple map layers that can be toggled on/off: Terrain (hillshading), Contour lines (elevation), Public transport stops, Places (POIs) with Wikipedia descriptions, Speed cameras, and Subway/tram lines.

Maps.me Comparison

Maps.me and Organic Maps share a common codebase (Maps.me was originally open-source, Organic Maps forked from it). Key differences:

  • Privacy: Organic Maps has zero tracking. Maps.me has optional analytics and account-based features (bookmark sync, hotel booking).
  • Features: Maps.me has public transit routing and hotel booking. Organic Maps focuses on core navigation and privacy.
  • Open Source: Both are open source, but Organic Maps is more community-driven with regular updates.
  • Maps update frequency: Both update monthly from OpenStreetMap data.

Bottom line: Use Organic Maps for privacy and simplicity. Use Maps.me if you need public transit routing in urban areas.

OsmAnd Advanced Features

OsmAnd is the most feature-rich offline map app. Beyond basic navigation, it offers these advanced capabilities:

  • GPX track recording — Record your route as a GPX track. Useful for hiking, mapping trails, or documenting travel routes for later sharing.
  • POI layers — Overlay points of interest (restaurants, fuel stations, hospitals, water sources) on the map. Search offline by category.
  • Altitude profiles — View elevation profiles for routes. Essential for hiking and cycling to gauge climb difficulty.
  • Contour lines & hillshade — Add-on that provides topographic information. Paid feature but available for free via F-Droid version.
  • Audio/video notes — Record audio notes attached to map locations. Useful for field research or documenting observations.
  • Nautical charts — Specialized charts for marine navigation. Includes buoys, depth markers, and harbor information.

Map Annotation & Bookmarking

Marking locations on offline maps is essential for emergency planning. Here is how to annotate maps in each app:

  • Organic Maps: Tap and hold on a location → Add Bookmark. You can categorize bookmarks (Home, Work, Favorite) and assign colors. Export all bookmarks as KML file for sharing.
  • OsmAnd: Create Favorites folders for different categories (Water Sources, Shelters, Meeting Points, Fallback Routes). Add descriptions to each favorite. OsmAnd supports GPX waypoints with custom icons and descriptions.
  • Maps.me: Bookmark locations with notes. Bookmarks sync if you have an account (but sync requires internet). Export bookmarks as KML for backup.

Topographic Maps for Hiking

Topographic maps show elevation contours, terrain features, trails, and water sources. They are essential for backcountry navigation and hiking.

  • OsmAnd — Contour lines and hillshade are available as a paid add-on (or free with F-Droid version). Download for the regions you plan to hike. Contour interval: 10m for mountainous areas, 25m for flat terrain.
  • Organic Maps — Contour lines are included for free. Enable them in the map layers menu. Suitable for most hiking needs.
  • Gaia GPS — Professional hiking app with USGS topo maps, satellite overlays, and MVUM (Motor Vehicle Use Maps). Download maps for offline use. Subscription required for premium features.
  • Paper maps — USGS 7.5-minute quadrangle maps (1:24,000 scale). The gold standard for serious hiking. Download and print before your trip. Store in a waterproof case.

Marine Charts

For boating, kayaking, and coastal navigation, specialized marine charts are available offline:

  • OsmAnd Nautical — Paid add-on with depth contours, buoys, lighthouses, harbors, and anchorage points. Covers global coastal waters.
  • OpenCPN — Professional marine navigation software for Windows/Linux/macOS. Supports S-57 ENC charts, AIS integration, weather routing. Runs entirely offline.
  • Navionics — Mobile chartplotter app. Download charts for offline use. Subscription required for full features.

Satellite Imagery Caching

Satellite imagery can be cached for offline use, providing a photographic view of terrain, buildings, and vegetation. This is useful for route planning and area reconnaissance.

  • OsmAnd — Supports online satellite tile sources (Bing, DigitalGlobe) for reference while online. Does not cache satellite tiles for offline use natively.
  • Mobac (Mobile Atlas Creator) — Desktop tool that downloads satellite tiles from various sources (Google Satellite, Bing Aerial, ESRI) and packages them for offline use on your phone. The best solution for offline satellite imagery.
  • Google Earth Pro — Desktop app that can cache satellite imagery for offline use. Limited caching area. Use Mobac for large areas.
# Download satellite imagery with Mobac:
# 1. Download Mobac from https://mobac.sourceforge.io/
# 2. Select "Bing Virtual Earth" or "ESRI Satellite" as source
# 3. Draw rectangle over area of interest
# 4. Select zoom levels 12-18 (higher = more detail)
# 5. Choose OSMAnd SQLite format
# 6. Generate → transfer to phone (Android/data/net.osmand.plus/files/)

Offline Geocoding & Sharing Coordinates

Geocoding (converting an address to coordinates) and reverse geocoding (converting coordinates to an address) are features that work entirely offline in dedicated map apps.

  • Organic Maps — Full-text offline search finds places by name, address, and category. Tap and hold to get coordinates in multiple formats (DD, DMS, UTM).
  • OsmAnd — Advanced offline search with fuzzy matching. Shows coordinates in multiple formats. Share coordinates via any messaging app as a text message.

# Decimal Degrees (DD): 40.7128, -74.0060
# Deg Min Sec (DMS): 40°42'46"N, 74°00'22"W
# UTM: 18T 583960 4507522
# What3Words: ///lazy.tower.career (requires internet)

# DD is the most universally understood

Emergency Location Sharing Without Cellular

When cellular networks are down, you can still share your location using alternative methods:

  • Meshtastic — LoRa radio devices can share GPS positions across the mesh network. Each Meshtastic node broadcasts its position periodically. View positions on the Meshtastic map.
  • APRS (Amateur Radio) — Ham radio operators can send and receive position reports via APRS on 144.390 MHz. Position packets include GPS coordinates, course, speed, and altitude.
  • Satellite messengers — Devices like Garmin inReach, ZOLEO, and Spot transmit GPS coordinates via satellite. Works anywhere with clear sky view. Requires subscription.
  • Pre-agreed meeting points — Establish GPS coordinates for emergency meeting points with your family. Memorize them or store in a waterproof card in your go-bag.

Map Updates Via USB

When internet is unavailable, you can still update your offline maps by physically transferring new map files via USB or SD card:

# 2. Copy to USB drive
# 3. Connect USB to Android device via OTG cable
# 4. Use file manager to copy .mwm files to Organic Maps data directory
# 5. Restart Organic Maps

# 3. Insert SD into phone
# 4. OsmAnd detects new maps and offers to move/install

Area Calculation & Trail Maps

Offline map apps can calculate areas and distances, which is useful for planning and emergency scenarios.

  • OsmAnd — Measurement tool for distances, areas, and perimeters. Tap points on the map to create a polygon and calculate its area. Useful for estimating land area, crop fields, or evacuation zones.
  • Organic Maps — Route distance calculation when planning a route. Shows total distance and estimated time for car, bike, and walking routes.
  • GPX tracks — Pre-download GPX tracks for popular trails from sites like AllTrails, Hiking Project, or Wikiloc. Import them into your offline map app before departure. Over 100,000 trails available for download.
