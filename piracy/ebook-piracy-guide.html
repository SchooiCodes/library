Ebook & Audiobook Piracy Guide

Everything for book pirates: Z-Library, Anna’s Archive, LibGen, MyAnonamouse (MAM), Readarr automation, and more.

Table of Contents

Z-Library

Z-Library is the largest library of digital books on the internet, with millions of ebooks and articles available for free. Domains have been seized multiple times. Access via the official I2P eepSite, Telegram bot (@zlib_bot), or Tor. Formats: EPUB, MOBI, PDF.

Anna’s Archive

Anna’s Archive is a meta-search engine that aggregates Z-Library, LibGen, Sci-Hub, and others. The most comprehensive book search available. The full metadata database is available for download as a torrent. No tracking, no JavaScript.

Library Genesis (LibGen)

LibGen is the oldest and most resilient book piracy archive. Content includes scientific articles, textbooks, fiction, non-fiction, comics. Access via libgen.is, libgen.rs, libgen.st. Advanced search filters by year, language, publisher, format.

MyAnonamouse (MAM)

MAM is the #1 private tracker for ebooks and audiobooks with over 500K ebooks and 100K audiobooks. Easy IRC interview process to join. Generous ratio system with bonus points. Active request system.

# MAM IRC interview:
# 1. Connect to irc.rizon.net via IRC client
# 2. Join #mam.interview
# 3. Read the topic and rules
# 4. Type !q to get a question
# 5. Answer honestly and politely
# 6. You’ll be invited after a brief chat

Readarr Automation

Readarr is the ebook/audiobook automation tool in the Arr stack. Add authors with quality profiles, it monitors for new releases, searches indexers, and sends to your download client. Supports MAM, NZBGeek, and Torznab indexers. Can integrate with Calibre for metadata management.

# Readarr + Calibre workflow:
# 1. Run Readarr (docker or native)
# 2. Add MAM as indexer via Prowlarr
# 3. Connect to download client (qBittorrent/SABnzbd)
# 4. Add authors you follow
# 5. Set quality profile (EPUB preferred)
# 6. Optionally connect to Calibre for library management

Z-Library Access Methods

Z-Library (Z-Lib) has had multiple domains seized by the FBI. Staying connected requires using their resilient access methods:

  • Tor access: Official .onion site (check r/zlibrary for current address). Requires Tor Browser. Fastest and most reliable method.
  • I2P eepsite: Accessible via the I2P network. Slower but more resilient than Tor. Good for countries that block Tor.
  • Telegram bot: @zlib_bot and @zlib_official_bot. Send book name or ISBN. Returns EPUB/MOBI/PDF. Daily download limits apply.
  • Personal bot: SingleLogin feature gives you a personal Telegram bot with higher daily limits. Login on the site and link your Telegram account.
  • Mirror sites: SingleLogin also works on all verified mirrors. Find current mirrors via Z-Lib’s official social media or r/zlibrary.
Note: Free accounts have daily download limits (5-10 books/day). Premium donations remove limits and add features like bulk downloads.

Anna’s Archive Deep Dive

Anna’s Archive is the most comprehensive book search engine, aggregating Z-Library, Library Genesis, Sci-Hub, and more into one searchable index.

  • Search features: Search by title, author, ISBN, or file hash. Advanced filters for format, year, language, file size.
  • Full metadata database: The complete metadata (40TB+) is available as torrents. You can run your own local mirror.
  • Download methods: Direct download from Anna’s Archive servers, IPFS, or partner mirrors. Some mirrors require free registration.
  • Privacy: No JavaScript required. No tracking. Access via Tor or I2P for additional privacy. The site itself recommends using a VPN.
  • Community: Open source project. Active development. Users can contribute metadata corrections and new sources.

Library Genesis for Textbooks

LibGen is the best source for academic textbooks, scientific literature, and educational materials. It is especially valuable for students who cannot afford expensive textbooks.

  • Mirrors: libgen.is, libgen.rs, libgen.st, libgen.li. If one is down, try another.
  • Search tips: Use ISBN for precise results. Filter by year for the latest edition. Filter by language for non-English content.
  • Non-fiction / fiction: LibGen also has a large fiction section. The comics section (LibGen Comics) has graphic novels and manga.
  • Scientific articles: LibGen includes millions of scientific papers. For papers not found, try Sci-Hub (which uses LibGen as a backend).
  • Download limits: LibGen has no strict limits, but some mirrors may throttle. Use a VPN if downloading large volumes.
# LibGen search via command line (using libgen_rs):
# python3 -m libgen_rs search "Introduction to Algorithms" --filter year:2022
# Download directly:
# python3 -m libgen_rs download "book_id"

Mobilism Forum

Mobilism is a community forum focused on ebooks, audiobooks, and mobile apps. It has one of the largest collections of user-uploaded ebooks.

  • Ebooks section: Thousands of user-uploaded ebooks in EPUB, MOBI, and PDF formats. Well-organized by genre.
  • Trusted uploaders: Look for uploaders with high post counts and positive reputation. "Best" rated releases are usually high quality.
  • Requests: If you cannot find a book, make a request. The community is very responsive.
  • Registration: Free registration required to download. No ratio system to maintain.
  • Modded apps: Mobilism also hosts modded APKs (audio players, ebook readers) that complement your reading experience.

AudiobookBay & Audiobook Trackers

AudiobookBay is the largest dedicated audiobook tracker (public). For private alternatives, MyAnonamouse has a massive audiobook collection.

  • AudiobookBay: Public tracker. All audiobooks are free. Search by author, narrator, or book title. Formats: MP3 and M4B.
  • MyAnonamouse (MAM): Private tracker with over 100K audiobooks. Better quality control and retention. Requires free IRC interview.
  • AudioBook Torrents (thegeek): Another private option. Smaller but curated collection.
  • BibSonomy / Hoopla: Some libraries offer free audiobook access. Check your local library for digital lending programs.

DeDRM Tools

DeDRM (Digital Rights Management removal) allows you to strip DRM from legally purchased ebooks so you can read them on any device or format.

  • Calibre + DeDRM Plugin: The standard approach. Install Calibre, add the DeDRM plugin (github.com/apprenticeharper/DeDRM_tools). Import Kindle books, Adobe Digital Editions books, or Kobo books. They are automatically decrypted.
  • Kindle for PC/Mac: An older version of Kindle for PC (1.17 or earlier) can be used with Calibre to strip Kindle DRM. Newer versions have hardened DRM.
  • Adobe Digital Editions: For EPUB PDFs with Adobe DRM. Authorize ADE, add the DeDRM plugin to Calibre, import the book.
  • Legal notice: DeDRM is legal in some jurisdictions for personal use where you own the content. Check your local laws.
# Calibre DeDRM setup:
# 1. Install Calibre from calibre-ebook.com
# 2. Download DeDRM_tools from GitHub
# 3. Calibre → Preferences → Plugins → Load plugin from file
# 4. Select DeDRM_plugin.zip
# 5. Enter your Kindle serial number (for Kindle books)
# 6. Import Kindle/ADE book - it decrypts automatically

MyAnonaMouse Private Tracker

MAM is the premier private tracker for ebooks and audiobooks. Its generous ratio system and active community make it the best place for book enthusiasts.

  • Joining: IRC interview process (see above). Study the rules carefully before the interview.
  • Ratio system: Bonus points for seeding. 1GB of upload = 1 bonus point. Bonus points can buy upload credit, VIP status, or freeleech tokens.
  • Request system: Very active. Users fill requests quickly, especially for popular books. Offer a bounty (upload credit) for faster fills.
  • Format preferences: EPUB is the most valued format. Audiobooks in M4B format are also well-received.
  • Seed requirements: Minimum 72 hours seeding time. After that, you can delete. But longer seeding earns more bonus points.

Sonarr/Readarr for Books

Automate your book collection with the same tools used for TV and movies:

  • Readarr: Dedicated ebook/audiobook automation. Add authors, set quality profiles (EPUB preferred, MOBI acceptable), connect to indexers (MAM, NZBGeek) and download clients. Monitors for new releases by authors you follow.
  • LazyLibrarian: Alternative to Readarr. Supports more indexers out of the box. Less polished UI but more flexible.
  • Calibre integration: Both Readarr and LazyLibrarian can send downloaded books to Calibre for automatic metadata management, format conversion, and library organization.
  • Download clients: qBittorrent for MAM torrents, SABnzbd for Usenet, or direct HTTP downloads for DDL sources.

Comic Book Downloading

Comics have their own ecosystem of piracy sources and formats.

  • GetComics: The best DDL site for comics and graphic novels. Well-organized by character, publisher, and story arc. Direct download links, no torrents needed.
  • Comic Book Plus: Public domain comics. Legal to download. Golden Age and Silver Age comics.
  • ReadComicOnline: Web-based comic reader. Large library. Use with adblocker.
  • Formats: CBZ (zip of images) and CBR (rar of images) are standard. Convert to PDF for easier reading on e-readers.
  • Automation: Mylar3 is an automated comic downloader (like Sonarr for comics). Monitors series, searches indexers, downloads new issues.

Manga Sources

Manga piracy is enormous. Here are the best sources and tools:

  • MangaDex: The largest manga reading site. Community translations, multiple languages. Free, no ads. Use Tachiyomi app for mobile reading.
  • Tachiyomi: Android manga reader app that aggregates sources (MangaDex, MangaPlus, 100+ others). Download for offline reading. Open source, no ads.
  • Nyaa.si: The torrent site has a dedicated manga section. Look for releases from trusted groups.
  • Manga4Life / MangaSee: Web-based alternatives. Use with adblocker and VPN.
  • HakuNeko: Desktop downloader for multiple manga and comic sources. Supports batch downloads and format conversion.

Academic Paper Access (Sci-Hub)

Sci-Hub provides free access to millions of scientific papers that are behind publisher paywalls. It is essential for researchers and students without institutional access.

  • Access: sci-hub.se, sci-hub.ru, sci-hub.st. If blocked, try the .onion address via Tor.
  • How to use: Paste a paper URL (DOI, PubMed ID, or full URL). Sci-Hub retrieves the PDF from its server or LibGen.
  • Browser extension: Sci-Hub plugin or "Sci-Hub X Now" redirects you automatically when you hit a paywall.
  • Limitations: Recent papers (<1 year old) are less likely to be available. Some publishers aggressively block Sci-Hub IPs.
  • Legal status: Sci-Hub has been sued and blocked in many countries. Use a VPN or Tor for access. Be aware of legal risks in your jurisdiction.
