Anime Piracy Guide — Streaming, Torrents & Fansubs

Complete guide to anime piracy: streaming sites, download groups (SubsPlease, Erai), Nyaa torrents, fansub groups, and automation with SeaDex.

Anime Streaming Sites

  • Aniwatch.to — Modern interface, fast servers, good community features.
  • 9anime / AniWave — Massive library with multiple servers. Use with uBlock Origin.
  • Animepahe, Zoro/AniMix, Gogoanime — Long-standing sites with extensive catalogs.
  • Miru Project — Desktop app that scrapes multiple sources. Anime-focused Stremio alternative.

Download Groups

  • SubsPlease — Gold standard for current-season simulcasts. High-quality 1080p x264.
  • Erai-raws — Multi-format, multi-subtitle releases. Excellent for streaming and archival.
  • Judas — High-quality releases for specific series.
  • EMBER — Focused on older and classic series.
  • BeatBox — Anime music and OST releases.

Nyaa Torrents

Nyaa.si is THE anime torrent site. Categories include Anime (English-translated), Raw, Audio, Literature, and Live-Action. Trusted uploaders have a shield icon. Filter by uploader, quality, and codec.

Fansubs

Fansub groups translate anime that official licensors may never localize. Notable groups: COMMIE, HorribleSubs (archived), Mazui, UTW, Doki, Kaleido, CoalGirls. Quality varies; read comments and reviews before downloading.

Automation with SeaDex

SeaDex (seadex.moe) is a community-maintained index that maps anime series to the best available releases. Use with Sonarr + Prowlarr + qBittorrent/SABnzbd for full automation.

# Settings → Profiles → Add new
# Name: Anime HD
# Language: Japanese (with English subtitles)
# Quality: 1080p, allow 720p as upgrade cutoff
# Preferred: SubsPlease, Erai-raws, Judas

Nyaa.si Deep Guide

Nyaa.si is the single most important site for anime torrents. Mastering it unlocks the full world of anime piracy.

  • Categories: Anime (English-translated), Raw (Japanese no subs), Audio (soundtracks, OP/ED), Literature (manga, light novels), Live-Action (Japanese TV/movies).
  • Trusted uploaders: Look for the shield icon next to uploader names. Trusted uploaders: SubsPlease, Erai-raws, Judas, EMBER, BeatBox, AnimeKaizoku, N4C.
  • Search filters: Use the advanced search: filter by category, uploader, quality (1080p, 720p), codec (x264, HEVC/x265), and file size range.
  • Batch vs individual: Batch torrents contain entire series. Individual torrents are per-episode. Batch is more efficient for completed series.
  • Comments: Read comments before downloading. Users report quality issues, subtitle sync problems, and fake torrents.
# Advanced Nyaa search URL examples:
# Search by uploader:
# https://nyaa.si/?f=0&c=0_0&q=&u=SubsPlease
#
# Search 1080p HEVC batch:
# https://nyaa.si/?f=0&c=0_0&q=1080p+HEVC+%5BBatch%5D
#
# RSS for specific show + uploader:
# https://nyaa.si/?page=rss&q=One+Piece+SubsPlease

SubsPlease Batches

SubsPlease is the gold standard for current-season anime releases. Their batches are released after a season completes.

  • Format: 1080p x264. Consistent quality across all episodes. English subtitles (.ass format, stylized).
  • Release schedule: Episodes 12-24 hours after Japanese broadcast. Batches 1-2 weeks after season finale.
  • Batch advantages: Single download for entire season. Consistent encoding. Smaller total size than individual episodes combined.
  • Quality: Excellent bitrate. No visual artifacts. Properly synced subtitles. SubsPlease is the preferred source for most anime automation setups.

Erai-Raws & AnimeTosho

Erai-raws provides multi-format, multi-subtitle releases excellent for both streaming and archival purposes.

  • Erai-raws: Releases in both x264 (compatible) and HEVC (smaller files). Multiple subtitle languages in each release. Good for non-English viewers.
  • AnimeTosho: A metadata and file-hosting service that mirrors Nyaa torrents and provides DDL links. Useful when torrents are stalled or you prefer direct download.
  • AnimeTosho DDL: Browse by series or search. Direct HTTP downloads of individual episodes or batches. No torrent client needed.
  • Integration: AnimeTosho has an API that Sonarr can use as an indexer for anime content.

Subtitle Management

Good subtitles are crucial for anime. Here is how to manage them:

  • Bazarr: Automated subtitle downloader that integrates with Sonarr/Radarr. Finds subtitles in multiple languages. Supports ASS, SRT, SUB formats.
  • Kitsunekko: Archive of fansub subtitles. Download individual .ass files for specific series. Best for getting high-quality translated subtitles for raw releases.
  • Subtitle sources: OpenSubtitles, Subscene, Addic7ed for anime. Anime-specific: kitsunekko.net, anime-subtitle.info.
  • Styling: .ass (Advanced SubStation Alpha) subtitles support styling, karaoke effects, fonts. .srt is plain but universal. Use .ass for authenticity.
  • Sync issues: If subtitles are out of sync, use Subtitle Edit to adjust timing. Manual sync or use the "Visual sync" tool.

Sonarr for Anime

Sonarr can be configured specifically for anime to handle the unique naming, release grouping, and quality preferences.

# Sonarr anime-specific settings:
# Settings → Indexers → Add Nyaa as Torznab indexer
# Settings → Profiles → Anime HD profile:
#   Language: Japanese (English subtitles preferred)
#   Quality: 1080p (allow upgrade from 720p)
#   Preferred tags: SubsPlease, Erai-raws, Judas, [Batch]
#
# Settings → Media Management:
#   Episode naming: {Series Title} - S{season:00}E{episode:00}
#   Use anime-specific naming: {Absolute Episode Number}
  • Indexers: Add Nyaa.si via Prowlarr (Torznab). AnimeTosho also works as an indexer.
  • Release profiles: Create preferred release groups (SubsPlease, Erai-raws, Judas) so Sonarr favors them.
  • Absolute numbering: Anime uses absolute episode numbers (Episode 1, 2, 3...) rather than S01E01 format. Enable in Sonarr settings.

Quality & Encoding Guide

Understanding video encoding helps you choose the right release for your needs and saves storage space.

Codec Efficiency File Size (24min episode) Compatibility Best For
x264 (H.264)Good350-500 MBUniversal (all devices)Compatibility, SubsPlease standard
HEVC (x265)Very good150-300 MBModern devices onlyStorage saving, 4K content
AV1Excellent100-200 MBLimited (new GPUs, software decode)Maximum compression, future-proof
  • 720p vs 1080p for anime: Unlike live-action, anime benefits less from 1080p due to cel-animated source material. 720p from a good encode can look nearly identical to 1080p on reasonable screen sizes.
  • HEVC advantage: For anime, HEVC typically achieves 40-50% smaller files than x264 at the same visual quality. Excellent for archiving entire series.
  • Mini encodes: Groups like Judas and Erai-raws produce HEVC mini encodes. Files are 100-200MB per episode with good quality.

Crunchyroll/Funimation Downloaders

If you have a premium subscription to legal streaming services, you can download and archive their content for offline use.

  • Crunchyroll Downloader: Several tools exist: crunchyroll-dl (CLI), Crunchyroll Downloader (GUI), and Yank (uses yt-dlp). Download episodes in up to 1080p with subtitles.
  • Funimation Downloader: Funimation-dl (CLI). Supports batch downloads of entire series. Requires free or premium Funimation account.
  • yt-dlp: Can download from Crunchyroll with cookies. Export your browser cookies and use: 
    yt-dlp --cookies cookies.txt "https://www.crunchyroll.com/series/..."
  • Legal note: Downloading from services you have a subscription to exists in a legal gray area. Check the terms of service.

Manga Reading Sites

Manga piracy has its own dedicated ecosystem of sites and apps:

  • MangaDex: The largest manga reading site. Community translations in dozens of languages. No official app but works perfectly in mobile browsers. Download chapters as CBZ for offline reading.
  • Tachiyomi: Android manga reader. Aggregates content from MangaDex, MangaPlus, Mangasee, and 100+ other sources. Download for offline reading. Open source, no ads, customizable.
  • HakuNeko: Desktop manga/comic downloader (Win/Mac/Linux). Download from multiple sources. Organize into chapter/volume structure.
  • MangaPlus by Shueisha: Official free app. First and last 3 chapters of most series are free. Legal, high quality.
  • ComicK: Modern manga reading site. Clean interface, fast loading, good for mobile.

AniList / MAL for Tracking

Keep track of what you have watched, are watching, and plan to watch with dedicated anime tracking services.

  • MyAnimeList (MAL): The largest anime and manga database. Rate, review, and track your progress. Community forums and recommendations.
  • AniList: Modern alternative to MAL. Cleaner interface, better API, no ads. Supports custom lists, tags, and advanced search.
  • AniChart: Seasonal anime chart. See what is airing each season. Filter by studio, genre, format. Direct links to streaming sources.
  • Taiga: Desktop app that automatically detects what you are watching and updates MAL/AniList. Integrates with media players like MPC-HC, VLC.
  • Shinden: Polish tracking platform. Also provides streaming links to subscribed users.

Notification Apps

Never miss a new episode with these notification tools:

  • AniLab: Android app for tracking and notifications. Automatically detects new episodes from streaming sites.
  • MyAnimeList app: Official MAL mobile app. Notifications for new episodes of tracked series.
  • Discord bots: Set up a Discord bot to notify your server when new episodes are released. Many public bots support anime RSS feeds.
  • RSS + Telegram: Use Nyaa RSS feeds + Telegram bots. Nyaa sends notifications to your Telegram when new episodes of tracked series are uploaded.
  • SeaDex RSS: SeaDex provides per-series RSS feeds for the best available release. Subscribe in your feed reader or automation tool.
