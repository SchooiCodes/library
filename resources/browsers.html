📄 Browser Recommendations

Recommendations for modern, privacy-focused web browsers — from everyday use to maximum anonymity.

🌐 Which Browser Should You Use?

Your browser is your gateway to the internet. Choosing the right one balances privacy, performance, and features:

  • Everyday use + privacy: Firefox or Brave
  • Maximum privacy out of the box: LibreWolf
  • Power users & customization: Vivaldi
  • Anonymity & bypassing censorship: Tor Browser
  • Chromium familiarity + built-in ad blocking: Brave

🦊 Firefox

Best for: Customization, extensions, and a good balance of privacy and usability.

  • Open-source (Gecko engine)
  • Wide extension library (uBlock Origin, Privacy Badger, etc.)
  • Enhanced Tracking Protection built in
  • DNS over HTTPS support
  • Container tabs for session isolation
  • Download Firefox

🛡️ Brave

Best for: Built-in ad blocking, privacy shields, and Chromium compatibility.

  • Blocks ads and trackers by default
  • Built-in Tor mode for private windows
  • HTTPS Everywhere built in
  • Brave Rewards (optional crypto)
  • Chromium-based — works with most Chrome extensions
  • Download Brave

🐺 LibreWolf

Best for: Maximum privacy out of the box, hardened Firefox fork.

  • Firefox fork with privacy hardening
  • Telemetry disabled by default
  • uBlock Origin pre-installed
  • Resistant fingerprinting
  • No Mozilla services (Pocket, Sync, etc.)
  • Download LibreWolf

🎨 Vivaldi

Best for: Power users who want deep customization.

  • Highly customizable UI
  • Built-in notes, screenshot tool, and calendar
  • Tab stacking and tiling
  • Chromium-based with strong privacy features
  • Download Vivaldi

🧅 Tor Browser

Best for: Anonymity, bypassing censorship, and sensitive research.

  • Routes all traffic through the Tor network (three layers of encryption)
  • No tracking — your activity cannot be traced back to you
  • Access .onion sites
  • Based on Firefox ESR
  • Note: Slower due to routing, not recommended for everyday browsing
  • Download Tor Browser
