Buying Guide

Tablet Buyer's Guide

iPad, Android, or Windows — choose the right tablet for creativity, productivity, or entertainment.

Beware of "Tablet" Scams: Many cheap Android tablets (< $100) advertised on Amazon or AliExpress run outdated Android versions, have malware pre-installed, and never receive security updates. Stick with Samsung, Lenovo (Tab series), or Amazon Fire if buying budget Android. For iPad, buy only from Apple, Best Buy, or authorized resellers.

Budget Tier — Under $350

Entry-level tablets for media consumption, light note-taking, and kids. Performance is adequate for streaming, web browsing, and e-reading but not heavy multitasking or gaming.

  • Amazon Fire HD 10 / Max 11 ($100–190) — Great for Prime Video, Kindle, and kids. Fire OS is limited but sideloading Google Play is possible.
  • Samsung Galaxy Tab A9+ ($200–280) — 11-inch 90 Hz display, quad speakers, decent build. The best budget Android tablet.
  • Lenovo Tab M11 / P12 ($180–300) — Pen included in some bundles. Good for note-taking on a budget.
  • iPad 10th Gen (refurbished) ($280–330) — A14 chip, USB-C, great app ecosystem. The best value in tablets if you can stretch to $329 new.

Mid-Range — $350 to $700

The sweet spot for students and professionals. These tablets handle note-taking, drawing, and productivity with ease. Most support active styluses and keyboard cases.

Premium Tier — $700+

Flagship tablets with the best screens, processors, and accessory ecosystems. These can replace a laptop for many workflows.

  • iPad Pro M4 (2024) — Ultra-thin, Tandem OLED display (1,600 nits peak), M4 chip. The gold standard for creative professionals. $999+.
  • Samsung Galaxy Tab S10 Ultra — 14.6" AMOLED, MediaTek Dimensity 9300+, included S Pen, DeX mode. Best for multitasking. $1,199+.
  • Microsoft Surface Pro 10 — Full Windows 11, Intel Core Ultra, can run any desktop app. Best for legacy software. $1,199+ (without keyboard).
Screen Size Guide: 8–9" is best for e-reading and compact gaming. 10–11" is the sweet spot for note-taking and media. 12–13" approaches laptop-replacement territory but is heavier. 14"+ (Tab S10 Ultra, iPad Pro 13) is excellent for split-screen multitasking but awkward for handheld use.

Stylus Support & Keyboard Cases

Stylus: iPad supports Apple Pencil (1st, 2nd gen, USB-C, and Pro) — the gold standard with near-zero latency. Samsung tablets include the S Pen in-box. Third-party styluses work on most Android tablets via USI 2.0 standard but lack pressure sensitivity in many apps.

Keyboard Cases: The iPad Magic Keyboard ($299–349) is expensive but has a great trackpad and floating hinge. Samsung's Book Cover Keyboard ($199–229) includes a trackpad and kickstand. Third-party options (Logitech Combo Touch, Brydge) are cheaper but heavier.

Storage, Cellular & Accessories to Budget For

Base storage on most tablets is 64–128 GB. If you take photos, download media, or use pro apps, pay for the 256 GB upgrade — most tablets lack SD card slots (especially iPads). Cellular models add $100–150 but allow true on-the-go connectivity. Budget an extra $50–200 for accessories: a case ($20–40), screen protector ($10–20), stylus ($30–130), and keyboard case ($50–350) can double your upfront cost.

Student Discounts: Apple offers education pricing (typically $100–150 off iPad + free AirPods during back-to-school). Samsung has an education store with similar discounts. Lenovo and Microsoft also offer academic pricing through ID.me or Student Beans verification.
